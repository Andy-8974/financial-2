CPP2 (Second Additional CPP) was introduced in 2024. For 2025: employees pay 4% on earnings between $68,500 and $73,200. Unlike base CPP (which gives a credit), CPP2 contributions are a full tax deduction — better treatment for higher earners. Total max CPP2 contribution: ~$188.
Each RRSP dollar reduces taxable income by $1. At a 43% combined marginal rate (Ontario), $10,000 RRSP saves ~$4,300 in taxes. Your 2025 RRSP limit is 18% of 2024 earned income, max $32,490. Check your CRA Notice of Assessment for unused room.

OAS is clawed back at 15¢ per $1 of net income above $90,997 (2024, indexed). Fully eliminated at ~$150,000. Strategies: draw RRSP early in low-income years, split pension income with spouse, use TFSA withdrawals (tax-free, don't count as income).
